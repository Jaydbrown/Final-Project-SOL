LocalDAO Whitepaper

LocalDAO: Transparent Neighborhood Investment Governance

Version 1.1 · May 1, 2026

1. Executive Summary

LocalDAO is a community-governance and treasury framework for neighborhood investment groups. It enables communities to form DAOs, fund local projects with stablecoins, vote transparently, and distribute project returns using auditable smart contracts.

2. Problem

  • Community investment decisions are often opaque and hard to audit.
  • Manual spreadsheets and chat records create disputes over contributions and payouts.
  • Leadership transitions can break continuity and trust.
  • Members have limited visibility into who approved decisions and when.

3. Solution

LocalDAO provides an on-chain operating model with clear roles and immutable records:

  • Factory deploys community DAOs as lightweight proxy instances.
  • Admins onboard members and create project proposals.
  • Members vote by staking USDC for support votes.
  • Finance leads deposit realized returns for distribution.
  • Members claim yields proportionally to their recorded stake.

4. Current Product Experience (Implemented)

  • Landing experience with guided sections: trust, features, governance, and active DAO discovery.
  • Dashboard with connected wallet visibility, portfolio entry points, and in-app navigation.
  • DAO messaging rooms with member labels, real-time sync, and unread tracking.
  • Notification center for vote-needed and claimable-yield alerts.
  • Profile and identity UX improvements to prioritize readable member names over raw wallet strings.
  • Whitepaper access from landing navigation and standalone document route.

5. Contract System

LocalDAOFactory

  • Creates DAO instances from a shared implementation.
  • Stores and exposes DAO registry metadata.

LocalDAO

  • Role management: creator, admin, finance manager, member.
  • Investment lifecycle: create, vote, activate, close, incomplete flow.
  • Treasury actions: vote stake, yield deposit, claim, and refund logic.
  • Activity logs and events for auditability.

6. Governance Model

  • Creator: appoints admins and finance leads, emergency pause control.
  • Admin: member operations and proposal lifecycle controls.
  • Finance lead: deadline extensions and yield deposits.
  • Member: voting, yield claims, and stake withdrawal where eligible.

7. Economic Flow

  • Support vote amount is denominated in USDC stake.
  • Yield is deposited on-chain after project outcome.
  • Payout formula allocates yield by stake proportion.
  • Unclaimed yield follows grace-period recovery rules.

8. Risks

  • Smart contract vulnerabilities and integration defects.
  • Role-key compromise and operational misuse.
  • Off-chain project execution and legal compliance constraints.

9. Roadmap (Near-Term)

  • Announcement module for DAO-wide updates with read tracking.
  • User profile backend and persistent identity preferences.
  • Production notification engine with preference controls and delivery history.
  • AI assistant for DAO onboarding, support, and governance guidance.
  • Security hardening: audit pipeline and privileged-role controls.

10. Conclusion

LocalDAO turns informal neighborhood investment coordination into a transparent, rules-driven system. It improves trust, accountability, and payout integrity by making governance and treasury activity verifiable on-chain.
